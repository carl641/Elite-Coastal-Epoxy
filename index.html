Our Proven 4-Step Process

Every floor gets the same professional-grade treatment

1

Diamond Grind Preparation

We use industrial diamond grinders to profile your concrete, removing contaminants and creating the ideal surface for a permanent mechanical bond. No shortcuts, no acid etching.

2

Repair & Prime

Cracks, spalls, and imperfections are filled and repaired. A high-penetrating primer is applied to seal the concrete and provide the foundation for our coating system.

3

Decorative Flake Application

Your chosen decorative flake or metallic pigment is broadcast into the base coat for a seamless, slip-resistant finish with depth and character unique to your floor.

4

Clear Polyaspartic Topcoat

A UV-stable, high-gloss polyaspartic topcoat seals everything in. This final layer provides chemical resistance, hot tire resistance, and a finish that stays beautiful for years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about epoxy and polyaspartic floor coatings

How long does installation take?

Most residential garage floors (2-3 car garages) are completed in a single day using our polyaspartic coating system. You can walk on the floor the same evening and park your car on it within 24 hours. Larger commercial projects may take 2-3 days depending on square footage and scope of work.

How much does epoxy flooring cost in Baldwin County?

Pricing depends on the size of your space, condition of the existing concrete, and the coating system you choose. A standard 2-car garage typically ranges from $1,800 to $3,500 for a full polyaspartic flake system. We provide free, no-obligation estimates so you'll know the exact cost before any work begins.

Will the coating peel or chip?

Not when installed correctly. The number one reason coatings fail is improper surface preparation. We use industrial diamond grinders — never acid etching — to create a mechanical bond profile on your concrete. Combined with our professional-grade polyaspartic resins, our coatings are backed by a 10-year warranty against peeling, flaking, and delamination.

Is polyaspartic better than epoxy?

For most residential and coastal applications, yes. Polyaspartic coatings cure much faster (allowing same-day use), are UV-stable (they won't yellow in sunlight), offer superior chemical and abrasion resistance, and perform better in extreme temperatures. They're also more flexible, which helps them handle the thermal expansion common in Gulf Coast climates.
